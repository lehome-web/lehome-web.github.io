  1. LeHome simulates Four Household Environments (kitchen, living room, washroom, and bedroom) with photorealistic assets and diverse interactive objects. These scenes cover a wide range of Daily Tasks such as folding clothes, cooking, and cleaning, providing a comprehensive platform for studying embodied manipulation in realistic home settings.

  2. LeHome focuses on Deformable Object manipulation, covering Six Physical Categories: plasmas, granular materials, linear objects, thin-shells, volumetric objects, and fluids. Using methods like PBD, FEM, and Dynamic Grids, it achieves high-fidelity simulations of elastic, plastic, and flow behaviors, enabling Realistic Interactions such as cutting, wrinkling, and pouring.

  3. LeHome introduces the Action Graph, a causal event-driven mechanism that links physical dynamics with logical task execution. This system ensures realistic cause-effect relationships in manipulation â€” such as a knife cutting a sausage or opening a microwave â€” by modeling triggers, computations, and state updates in a structured graph.

  4. LeHome supports Low-Cost & Open-Source robots (LeRobot, XLeRobot, LeKiwi). It supports keyboard, game controller, and leaderâ€“follower teleoperation, enabling easy data collection and high-fidelity teleoperation demonstrations. This combination offers an affordable, scalable solution for developing and testing household robotic policies.
