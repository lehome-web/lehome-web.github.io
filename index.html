A Simulation Environment for Deformable Object Manipulation in Household Scenarios

Video

Highlights

01 Household Scenes
LeHome simulates Four Household Environments (kitchen, living room, washroom, and bedroom) with photorealistic assets and diverse interactive objects. These scenes cover a wide range of Daily Tasks such as folding clothes, cooking, and cleaning, providing a comprehensive platform for studying embodied manipulation in realistic home settings.
02 Deformable Physics
LeHome focuses on Deformable Object simulation and manipulation, covering Six Physical Categories: Liquid, Gaseous Fluid, Granular Object, Linear Object, Thin Shell, and Volumetric Object. Using methods like PBD and FEM, it achieves high-fidelity simulations of elastic, plastic, and flow behaviors, enabling Realistic Interactions such as cutting, wrinkling, and pouring.
03 Action Graph
LeHome introduces the Action Graph, a causal event-driven mechanism that links physical dynamics with logical task execution. This system ensures realistic cause-effect relationships in manipulation, such as a knife cutting a sausage or opening a microwave, by modeling triggers, computations, and state updates in a structured graph.
04 Robot Support
LeHome supports Low-Cost and Open-Source robots. It supports keyboard, game controller, and leader-follower teleoperation, enabling easy data collection and high-fidelity teleoperation demonstrations. This combination offers an affordable, scalable solution for developing and testing household robotic policies.
